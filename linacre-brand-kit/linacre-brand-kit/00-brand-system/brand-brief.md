# Brand Brief — Linacre.site

## 1. What The Brand Is

**Linacre.site** is a personal engineering portfolio and toolkit hub for a full-stack and AI engineer. It should feel like a trusted builder's workspace: practical, warm, technical, and quietly premium.

It is not just a portfolio. It is a living system containing:

- a full-stack engineering identity,
- a curated directory of useful tools,
- a roadmap for self-taught developers,
- an AI sandbox/lab feeling,
- a changelog-driven product mindset,
- a personal brand that can scale across website, GitHub, LinkedIn, X, YouTube, and short-form video.

## 2. Brand Positioning

> Linacre.site helps self-taught builders, developers, and AI-curious makers discover practical tools, understand full-stack workflows, and follow the build journey of a real engineer.

The brand should sit between:

- **developer portfolio** and **toolkit directory**,
- **retro terminal** and **modern AI interface**,
- **personal craft** and **premium technical product**,
- **quiet confidence** and **experimental energy**.

## 3. Audience

### Primary Audience
Self-taught developers, junior engineers, tech learners, indie hackers, AI builders, and people looking for a practical roadmap into modern web development.

### Secondary Audience
Recruiters, collaborators, founders, clients, and other engineers who want to quickly understand your taste, skill, stack, and ability to build polished products.

## 4. Emotional Target

The audience should feel:

- “This person knows what they are doing.”
- “This is technical, but approachable.”
- “The site feels modern without being noisy.”
- “There is a personal signature here.”
- “I want to bookmark this.”

## 6. Brand Promise

> Useful tools, clean systems, and calm engineering guidance for people building their way into tech.

## 7. Brand Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Practical Utility
The site must always be useful. The toolkit, roadmap, and prompts should make visitors feel like they have found a high-quality resource.

### Pillar 2 — Calm Technical Taste
Design should show restraint. Use glow, pulse, and cyber details sparingly. The premium feeling comes from spacing, clarity, and consistency.

### Pillar 3 — Human Builder Energy
The brand should not feel like a faceless AI company. It should feel like David Linacre's workshop: personal, honest, iterative, and real.

### Pillar 4 — Retro-Modern Signature
Retro influence should appear through warm amber, Rhodes/piano audio, subtle grid patterns, terminal details, and soft gradients. Modern influence should appear through clean UI, AI lab language, motion, and precise technical copy.

## 8. One-Line Descriptions

Use these in different places:

- **Website hero:** Full-stack and AI engineering, built with calm systems, useful tools, and amber-coded craft.
- **Social bio:** Full-stack + AI engineer. Building Linacre.site: tools, roadmaps, experiments, and clean web systems.
- **GitHub bio:** Full-stack and AI engineer building practical tools, polished interfaces, and self-taught developer resources.
- **LinkedIn headline:** Full-Stack & AI Engineer | React, TypeScript, Express, AI tooling | Creator of Linacre.site
- **Short intro:** I build useful full-stack and AI tools with a calm retro-modern design signature.

## 9. What The Brand Should Not Be

Avoid:

- harsh cyberpunk,
- aggressive neon,
- noisy EDM/gamer energy,
- generic SaaS blue gradients,
- cold corporate stock design,
- too many effects at once,
- fake futurism,
- overpromising AI hype,
- cluttered social graphics,
- music that distracts from the content.

## 10. Brand North Star

Every piece of Linacre.site should answer this:

> Does this feel useful, calm, technical, warm, and distinctively amber-coded?

If yes, it belongs. If not, simplify it.
