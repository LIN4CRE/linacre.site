# Tone of Voice — Linacre.site

## 1. Voice Personality

Linacre.site should sound like a calm engineer who has done the work and wants to make the path clearer for others.

The tone is:

- clear,
- practical,
- warm,
- quietly confident,
- curious,
- builder-focused,
- non-hype,
- slightly technical but not gatekeeping.

## 2. Voice Formula

```text
Practical engineering clarity
+ calm encouragement
+ concise technical detail
+ personal builder energy
= Linacre.site voice
```

## 3. Writing Rules

### Do

- Explain why something matters.
- Use plain English before jargon.
- Be specific about tools and outcomes.
- Sound human and direct.
- Use short headings.
- Use developer language where it adds credibility.
- Show progress through changelogs, experiments, and lessons learned.

### Avoid

- empty AI hype,
- “revolutionary” language,
- over-polished corporate phrasing,
- buzzword stacks without explanation,
- pretending everything is perfect,
- shouting with too many exclamation marks.

## 4. Example Tone

### Too corporate
> We deliver cutting-edge AI-enabled digital transformation solutions for scalable modern experiences.

### Better
> I build full-stack and AI tools that are practical, fast, and easy to understand.

### Too casual
> I mess around with code and AI stuff.

### Better
> I experiment with AI and full-stack systems, then turn the useful parts into clean tools and guides.

## 5. Key Phrases

Use these as reusable brand phrases:

- Calm systems for modern builders.
- Amber-coded full-stack craft.
- Practical tools for self-taught engineers.
- Build notes, useful links, and AI experiments.
- A quieter route into modern web development.
- Clean interfaces, useful systems, real experiments.
- Late-night coding energy, polished for the web.
- Retro warmth, modern engineering.

## 7. Caption Style

Use this structure:

1. Hook: one clear idea.
2. Detail: what you built, learned, or tested.
3. Value: why it helps another builder.
4. CTA: ask a simple question or invite people to visit.

### Example
> I rebuilt my portfolio as more than a portfolio.  
> It now works as a toolkit directory, roadmap, AI sandbox, and changelog-driven project hub.  
> The goal is simple: make the self-taught developer path easier to navigate.  
> What would you add to the toolkit?
