# Brand Rules — Linacre.site

## 1. The Three-Second Test

Someone should understand the brand in three seconds:

1. This is technical.
2. This is personal and useful.
3. This has a warm amber retro-modern signature.

If a design does not communicate those points, simplify it.

## 2. The 80/15/5 Rule

Use this visual balance:

- **80%** calm dark interface, readable content, useful structure.
- **15%** amber glow, hexagon, terminal detail, retro warmth.
- **5%** experimental cyber/AI accents such as cyan, violet, glitch, pulse.

This prevents the brand from becoming too loud.

## 3. Logo Rules

Do:

- Use the hex/pulse mark on dark backgrounds.
- Keep enough padding around the logo.
- Use amber, cream, black, or monochrome versions.
- Use the mark alone for small spaces.

Do not:

- Stretch the logo.
- Put the logo on busy backgrounds.
- Add extra shadows or effects not in the brand system.
- Use random colours.
- Place small detailed logo versions at favicon size.

## 4. Colour Rules

- Amber is the signature. Do not replace it with generic blue.
- Cyan/violet are rare accents, mainly for AI/lab moments.
- Use warm cream for premium contrast.
- Keep backgrounds dark and calm.
- Light mode can use cream backgrounds with ink text and amber accents.

## 5. Motion Rules

- Motion should be slow enough to feel premium.
- Hover effects should be subtle.
- Use pulse and glow, not shaking or spinning.
- If motion distracts from reading, remove it.

## 6. Audio Rules

- Audio is optional and must be user-controlled on the website.
- Never autoplay loud audio.
- Use the audio brand mostly in social intros, screen recordings, demo videos, and optional website loops.
- The brand sound is soft retro easy-listening with modern digital pulse.

## 7. Social Rules

- Keep every post visually recognisable: dark/amber, clear heading, one idea.
- Avoid cluttered carousels.
- Use consistent margins and repeated components.
- Use code screenshots only when readable.
- Always connect posts back to building, tools, AI, or learning.

## 8. Website Rules

- The site must load fast.
- Accessibility matters more than effects.
- Cards need clear hierarchy.
- The toolkit should remain searchable and useful.
- Changelog entries should feel like product releases.
- The AI Lab should feel experimental but safe and clear.

## 9. Final Brand Filter

Before publishing anything, ask:

- Is it useful?
- Is it calm?
- Is it technical?
- Is it warm?
- Is it recognisably Linacre.site?
